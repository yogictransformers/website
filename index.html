About us

Who are we (Yogic Transformers)?

A community STEAM program in Ashburn, VA — run by students, for students, with experienced coaches alongside.

Meet the masterminds behind your camper's summer: a league of extraordinary high school and college friends who spent the last decade conquering World Robotics stages, crushing STEM Olympiads, and coaching over 400 future innovators—all while our family collectively raised over $200K (of that our boys raised $40k) for educational causes. When the tight job market threw them a curveball this summer, they did what they do best: joined forces to transform obstacles into opportunities!

The result? The ultimate, budget-friendly, hands-on STEAM playground featuring customizable, weekly themed camps where kids can mix and match their adventures. It's a massive win-win: elite, world-class mentorship for your budget, and an unforgettable summer of explosive, creative fun for your kids. Your campers won't just learn STEAM—they'll catch the contagious energy of real-world makers who know exactly how to turn big dreams into reality. And maybe even start their journey into being a Yogic Transformer too!

The program

What is the STEAM Camp about?

Hands-on building across six STEAM areas — plus well-being and optional competition prep.

Every session is project-driven. Students build robots, code games and apps, engineer and 3D-print designs, create art, and grow as leaders and communicators — then learn to slow down and focus.

Work spans six areas: Robotics, Programming, Engineering, Design & Creativity, Leadership & Communication, and Competition Prep (TSA and Science Olympiad, where students pick their own event). Browse the full catalog in Activity Topics below.

Robotics Programming Engineering Design & Creativity Leadership & Comm. Competition Prep

Woven throughout: a Sudarshan Kriya (SKY) Youth Program for focus and well-being.

Our purpose

Why are we conducting this camp?

Great learning, real well-being, and a give-back to the community.

Kids learn STEAM best from people only a few years ahead of them. We put students in front of the room and experienced adults beside them, so curiosity stays high and support stays close.

Technical skill isn't enough on its own. We pair building and competing with well-being — focus, breathing, and life skills — so students leave more capable and calmer.

And it's a give-back: the students teaching here are raising funds for local schools and non-profit causes, so every registration sends help back into the community.

Give-back

How are we paying it forward?

You choose where a portion of the proceeds goes.
